About Me

I'm a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in Linguistics at The University of Ghent, working on the CAUSALITY project. Before that, I was a Research Fellow in Linguistics at Goethe University and a Postdoctoral Research Assistant in Computer Science at The University of Manchester. I completed my PhD in Linguistics at The University of Manchester, funded by the ESRC.

Broadly speaking, I'm interested in how meaning works in natural language and how compositional semantics and contextual factors interact. I use a variety of methods to investigate how meanings emerge both during online processing and diachronically over time as languages naturally change and evolve. I use a variety of methods, including carrying out behavioural experiments and building formal and computational models of meaning-making.

To find out more about me, take a look at my CV or my Slides & Posters. You may also be interested in my Free Materials.
