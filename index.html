About

I am an ESRC-funded PhD researcher in Linguistics and a Graduate Teaching Assistant in Linguistics and Psychology at The University of Manchester (UK). I am Supervised by Dr. Vera Hohaus and Prof. Andrew Stewart.

Broadly speaking, I'm interested in the way in which people get meaning from written text. My work falls into two main strands:

  1. The use of behavioural methods like semantic priming, eye-tracking and reading time measures to 'get at' (some of) the hidden semantic processes that are at play when we understand sentences.

  2. Developing, and building on existing, semantic models that capture theoretically relevant insights from formal semantics while providing a plausible mechanistic processing model based on empirical data collected in experimental tasks.

To find out more about my work, take a look at my CV or my Slides & Posters.
